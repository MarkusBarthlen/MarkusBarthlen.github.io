Oxygen

December 15, 2020 2-minute read

Oxygen by Nick Lane

I recently came across the book “Oxygen” by Nick Lane after watching the youtube channel SciManDan. This book provides both an overview of the discovery of the element, its historical importance, the changes of oxygen levels in the atmosphere and its influence on the metabolism of prokaryotes and eukaryotes. Interestingly, historical oxygen levels are not known without doubt. There is also no evidence that oxygen ever caused a mass extinction of anaerob organisms. While there are theories as to why the temperature on Earth and (possibly but not certainly) the oxygen levels varied, there is no hard evidence. Note that the oxygen levels on Earth are important for the retention of water. In fact, it is not even known when the first oxygen producing organisms first appeared on Earth. What is interesting is that oxygen and non-oxygen consuming cells share some mechanisms (antioxidants, (bacterio-)chlorophyll) to protect themselves and to gain energy. Too much oxygen, an infection or environmental stress can all result in free radicals in a cell.

Other interesting questions in the book:

  • Why was there an explosion of multicellular life in the Cambrian?
  • Can antioxidants slow down aging and cancer? When do they signal the body to stop producing its own antioxidants?
  • Do factors that increase lifespan reduce fecundity?
  • Why are bird mitochondria so efficient?
  • Why are sperm produced through life, but eggs are produced early in development and last a lifetime?
