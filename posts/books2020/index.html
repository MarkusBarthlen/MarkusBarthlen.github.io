Best books I read in 2020

  1. Almanack of Naval Ravikant by Eric Jorgenson

Easy to read and full of good quotes. I especially loved the parts about happiness. You might not even recognize that happiness is a valid goal in life. And it can have enormous impact on other aspects of your life. It’s free https://www.navalmanack.com. The best book I’ve ever read.

  1. Oxygen by Nick Lane

A tremendous book that will change your perspective on cell biology and possibly make you think about how you can live your life to better protect your cells. My main takeaway was that mitochondria are much more important than I previously thought.

  1. The beginning of infinity by David Deutsch

A very nice book on the philosophy of science. It made me think about where my knowledge and beliefs actually come from and the consequences of pessimism.

  1. Sapiens by Yuval Noah Harari

An eye opener that does a good job explaining the most important developments and breakthroughs in history.
How did the small country of the Netherlands become one of the most powerful countries?
How did 550 Spaniards conquer the Aztec empire of millions?

  1. Tools of Titans/Tribe of Mentors by Tim Ferris

A potpourri of interviews with a lot of potentially life changing ideas. My immediate takeaway was that I should pursue Zen meditation.

Bonus:

  • The little book of stoicism by Jonas Salzgeber

    Contains advice from some of the best philosophers of the Roman Empire.

  • Motiviert studiert by Daniel Hunold and Mirjam Reiß (alternatively for non-German speakers: Limitless by Jim Kwik)

    A lot of tips and tricks on how to improve your learning. Highly recommended.

  • Mitochondria and the future of medicine by Lee Know

    Goes into more detail about the Electron Transport Chain in mitochondria.
